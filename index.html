GASAN

Gasan is a coastal municipality in the island province of Marinduque. It is the province's third-largest town in terms of population, and is often called the cultural capital of Marinduque.

St. Joseph Spouse of Mary Parish

The St. Joseph Spouse of Mary Parish Church, also known as the Gasan Church, is a Roman Catholic church in the municipality of Gasan, Marinduque, Philippines. It is the seat of the parish of the same name under the Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Capiz. The church is a popular pilgrimage site and is known for its intricate carvings, beautiful interiors, and panoramic views.

Tres Reyes Island

Tres Reyes Islands, also known as the Three Kings Islands, is a group of three islands located in the municipality of Gasan, Marinduque, Philippines. The islands are named after the Three Kings of the Nativity: Gaspar, Melchor, and Baltazar. The islands are a popular tourist destination, known for their beautiful beaches, clear waters, and abundant marine life. The islands are also home to a number of caves and historical sites.

Gasan Sea View Park (Reyes Park)

Gasan Sea View Park is a public park located in the municipality of Gasan, Marinduque, Philippines. It is a popular tourist destination, offering stunning views of the Sibuyan Sea and the Tres Reyes Islands. The park is also home to a number of amenities, including a playground, a picnic area, and a viewing deck.

Our Culture

Kalutang

Kalutang is a traditional musical instrument from Marinduque, Philippines. It is made of two wooden bars that are struck together to produce sound. The bars are made of the kwatingan tree, which is native to Marinduque. The bars are of different sizes, and the larger bars produce lower notes. The kalutang is played by striking the bars with the hands or with a mallet. It is a percussion instrument, and it is often used in folk music and dance.

Festival

The Gasang-Gasang Festival is an annual festival held in the town of Gasan, Marinduque, Philippines.
